Reviewer critique (<=400 words):
The manuscript presents a clear mechanism-first framing of LLM-enhanced reinforcement learning as mixed-supervision trajectory optimization. The operator abstraction (planner, reward/verifier, world/generator, tool/memory) and the authority/arbitration dimension are well chosen and enable cross-paper synthesis without over-indexing on model branding. The figures are readable and the main taxonomy tables are placed near discussion, which supports survey usability.

However, the paper still reads unevenly across sections in terms of evidentiary support and actionable guidance. Citation integration is strong in a few anchor paragraphs and tables, but some subsections rely on generic statements with limited distributed citations. For a Q1 survey, each taxonomy subsection should sustain a dense trail of representative works tied to specific architectural design patterns, typical evaluation setups, and recurring failure modes. The evaluation narrative is improving, but would benefit from more concrete checklists and stress-test protocols that map directly to the operator view and authority schedules. Similarly, failure modes are described at a systems level, but the manuscript can do more to connect them to diagnostics and rollback logic that practitioners can implement.

Theoretical anchoring is directionally correct (shaping, weighting, model bias), but could be integrated more explicitly into the arbitration recipes and evaluation guidance. Finally, the case-study mapping matrix is a strong idea, yet it should more directly encode domain, arbitration policy, and failure checks in a compact, non-overflowing format.
